## Two Buyer Personas

### Persona A — The Founder / Brand Builder
- **Who:** Tech founders, startup CMOs, solo entrepreneurs, agency owners
- **Need:** A brandable, memorable, aged domain that communicates their mission instantly
- **Pain point:** Good .com domains are extinct; they're tired of awkward hyphenated names
- **Decision driver:** SEO trust signals + brand equity + competitor lockout
- **Content trigger:** "The struggle to find a premium domain"

### Persona B — The Kuwait Market Entrant
- **Who:** Kuwaiti entrepreneurs, expat founders in Kuwait, international companies expanding to Kuwait
- **Need:** A domain that signals "local" while keeping an international identity
- **Pain point:** Generic domains don't resonate with the Kuwaiti audience; they need the "Q8" shorthand
- **Decision driver:** Instant country recognition + cultural resonance + premium positioning
- **Content trigger:** "Building a brand that speaks to Kuwait"

## Tone of Voice Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Speak with authority | Use hype words ("game-changer", "revolutionary") |
| Be educational | Be salesy or pushy |
| Use clean, minimal visuals | Cluttered, busy designs |
| Mention "€20,000" confidently (signals premium) | Apologise for or justify the price |
| Reference Kuwait with cultural respect | Use stereotypes or lazy tropes |
| Use "#Communiq8" hashtag consistently | Overuse hashtags (>15 per post) |

---

## Visual Identity Guidelines

- **Colours:** Deep navy (#0F172A), gold accent (#F59E0B), clean white (#FFFFFF)
- **Typography:** Inter (sans-serif) for body, Playfair Display for headlines
- **Style:** Clean, premium, minimalist. Dark backgrounds with gold accents.
- **Imagery:** Abstract communication lines, network nodes, Kuwait skyline silhouettes, tech-forward aesthetics
- **Logo treatment:** "Communiq" in light weight, "8" in bold gold
